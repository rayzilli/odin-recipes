Patches Delight

Another lost bet, another shoe eaten

Top Gun fan Matt Patches said he would eat his shoe if a sequel was made and ten years later he did. Taking a more lighthearted approach to shoe eating this home-made shoe is sure to delight all and is the perfect recipe for children get started with cooking.

Ingredients

  • Two large rolls of fruit leather. These can be any flavor but we recommend strawberry. Home made fruit leather works as well.
  • Edible glue (available in baking section of most reputable grocery stores).
  • One medium package of licorice for the laces.
  • Paper for tracing and pencil
  • Hole punch or sharp pointed object similar size

Steps

  1. Trace sole of shoe on paper and cut out.
  2. Trace and cut out both sides of uppers.
  3. Place fruit leather on cut to size.
  4. Repeat with uppers.
  5. Use glue to attach sole to uppers.
  6. Use more fruit leather and glue to attach both sides. Get creative!
  7. Punch holes where laces go (3 each side is enough).
